Which Tea Is Right For You?
Please, Read The Description That Corresponds To The Right Tea!

Hung Cha - Black Tea

Free

Black tea has a long history in Chinese culture and is often served with dim sum. This tradition adds to the overall experience of enjoying dim sum. Black tea doesn't get overwhelmed by the diverse flavors of dim sum. The caffeine in black tea can provide a gentle energy boost to help you stay awake.

Po Lei Cha - Pu'er Tea

Free

Po Lei Cha, it's a unique green tea-based beverage that originates from Hakka cuisine. It's made by grinding a mixture of tea leaves, herbs, nuts, and seeds into a fine powder, then adding hot water to create a fragrant, slightly bitter drink. Po Lei Cha is known for its health benefits and distinct flavor. Po Lei Cha alongside the various dim sum dishes for a refreshing beverage choice.

Gok Fa Cha - Chrysanthemum Tea

Free

Chrysanthemum tea has a mild and refreshing floral flavor with a slightly sweet undertone. Chrysanthemum tea is known for its pleasant aroma, Chrysanthemum tea is often believed to help with digestion. Some people enjoy it during or after a meal to help with digestion, making it a suitable choice for a dim sum.

Mot Lei Fa Cha - Jasmine Tea

Free

Jasmine tea has a delicate and slightly sweet floral aroma and taste, which pairs well with the savory and often bold flavors of dim sum. Jasmine tea typically contains caffeine, which can provide a subtle energy boost during your meal. the warmth of the tea can aid in digestion, making it a good choice when enjoying a meal as rich and varied as a dim sum feast.

Wu Long Cha - Oolong tea

Free

Oolong teas are known for their fragrant aroma, the aroma of the tea can add an extra layer of enjoyment to the meal. Having Oolong tea with dim sum is a longstanding tradition. Oolong tea contains a moderate amount of caffeine, which can provide a gentle energy boost to keep you alert and awake.
